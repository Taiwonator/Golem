Waiting on God
is a profound spiritual practice that involves trusting in His timing and plan,
even amidst uncertainty or difficulty. It means patiently relying on God, being
steadfast in prayer, following His commandments, and doing His will while
expecting His promises to manifest. This requires hope and a positive attitude,
anchored in the assurance that God is faithful to His Word. The Bible
emphasizes that God's Word is unchanging and eternal (Isaiah 26:13; Psalm
29:2), and His promises will never fail.

Unlike humans,
who may change their minds or break promises, God is immutable and truthful.
Human understanding of waiting often hinges on personal timelines, leading to
frustration or doubt when expectations are unmet. However, God’s perspective is
vastly different. As 2 Peter 3:8 reminds us, “a thousand years is like a day”
to God, highlighting the need to align our patience with His divine timeframe.

Waiting on God
often involves growth and preparation. For example, someone may anticipate a
promise being fulfilled in two years, but God may have a ten-year plan. This
extended period allows for personal refinement through tests, trials, and
challenges, shaping character and developing the maturity needed to handle the
blessing. Biblical examples like Abraham, Joseph, and Moses demonstrate how
waiting on God leads to transformative experiences that ultimately fulfil His
promises. The Israelites’ 40-year journey through the wilderness (Deuteronomy
8:2) exemplifies how God uses waiting periods to refine character and deepen
dependence on Him.

God’s delays
are not denials. His timing is perfect, ensuring that His blessings do not
become curses due to pride or spiritual unpreparedness. Although humans often
desire immediate results, God’s ways are higher than ours (Isaiah 55:8-9).
Embracing this truth requires faith and willingness, as well as reliance on
God’s grace to endure trials.

Jesus
emphasized the importance of seeking God’s Kingdom first (Matthew 6:33). This
principle assures believers that as they prioritize God’s ways, He will meet
their needs. Psalm 62:1 reflects this trust: “Truly my soul waits upon God;
from him comes my salvation.” During the waiting period, maintaining hope and
trust is crucial, as expressed in verse 8: “Trust in him at all times, you
people; pour out your hearts before him.”

Ultimately,
God’s timing is always perfect. Jesus’ birth occurred at the “fullness of
time,” as ordained by God. Likewise, the restoration of Israel and the end of
the age remain within God’s sovereign timeline (Acts 1:6; Matthew 24:36).
Waiting on God requires patience, perseverance, and faith. As Isaiah 40:31
assures us, “those who wait on the Lord shall renew their strength.” Trusting
in God’s timing allows us to experience His faithfulness and prepares us to
receive His promises with gratitude and humility.
