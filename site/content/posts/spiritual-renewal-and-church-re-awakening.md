I have always
pondered in my mind the fact that the Church seems to be powerless today. It
has very little or no appeal to this generation that is highly materialistic.
As a member of the Church, at times I wonder why I seems to be of little use so
far to God in soul winning. That is just one among the myriads of dilemmas I
struggle with in my own Christian life.  I
am always hunted by the fact that my life like that of many other Christians
that claims to be Spirit filled is not always full of joy and making exploits
for Jesus.  Many times, I have prayed to
God to give me this joy and peace like he gave to the early disciples such as
Peter, Paul, Silas, John etc. God soon enabled me to understand that the fruits
of the spirits which these people are full of are not automatically conferred
on believers. God asked me whether I am walking with Him as the early disciples
walked with Him? I off course know that the answer is No. It was as if a veil
was removed from my face and suddenly, I realised that for me to have what they
had, I must walk with God as they walked.

Because God is
truth and forever consistent, He said something similar to me early this
morning before I came out of bed for morning devotion at 6am. The night before,
I had been reading the book authored by David Watson on Discipleship. David had
been lambasting the Western Christians for their highly materialistic approach
to the Christian life. He was not surprised that the Church lacks the power to
effectively evangelise the lost today and that when an effort is made from a
sense of duty, it is to say the least futile because the words are devoid of
the power of God and as such, they are unable to impress or convict the
unbeliever. He also mentioned the fact that people out there see nothing at all
that they can identify with among professing Christians. They are not impressed
or attracted at all to us. They have been able to see many so-called believers
as hypocrites. Do you blame them? Sinners love Sinners passionately because
they all do the same thing. Do Christians truly love fellow Christians. We
profess one thing, and we often do the opposite. Have many Christian Brethrens today
been able to demonstrate true love to one another as the early Church did and
their numbers grew rapidly? The Bible say they were in one accord and
fellowshipped together (Acts 2:1). By fellowship is meant they prayed together,
shared their food together, helped one another (Acts 4: 32 - 35). All these
things they did together were very visible to the unbelievers of their time.
What was the expected result of their way of life? Was God pleased? Yes, God
was pleased and the Holy Spirit worked powerfully amongst them confirming his
presence with them with great signs, miracles and wonders. The people of
Antioch were amazed to see these striking similarities in the life pattern of
Jesus and the early Christians that they called them "Christians" (Acts
11:19-26). I.e.  "Christ followers".
God is the same, yesterday, tomorrow and forever. It is us who are not serving
God in truth and spirit like the people of old did.

The truth be
said, many of us Christians are far from being 'Spirit filled', but we are
quick to claim that we are 'Spirit filled'. How can worldly, materialistic and self-seeking
people be Spirit filled at the same time as being worldly filled? If anybody is
truly spirit filled, his life will radiate the presence and glory of God.
People, even the unbelieving world will be drawn to this glory. They will be
attracted to his shining light. Miraculous things will happen. Sinners will be
converted to Christianity. Remember Stephen, Phillip, Peter, Paul and others
were all Spirit filled.  We need to study
the lives of these Apostles in other to appreciate the remarkable difference
between their lifestyles and accomplishment relative to that of modern-day
Christians; especially many professing to be 'Pastors & Apostles' today.
The truth as far as I am concerned is that I am not 'Spirit filled' in the
sense that the early Apostles were, but desires to be 'Spirit filled'. I am not
ashamed of this fact, and it is on this basis that I have been crying out to
God to weed out all the impurities in my life. The Holy Spirit cannot fill a
dirty and un yielded vessel.  I am
praying to God to release me from every weight that are hindering my walk with
Him. We can be what the Apostles of old were. We can feel the same presence of
God they felt and impact our world for Christ if only we are ready to pay the
price they paid. Theirs was a life of self denial and absolute surrender. They
presented their bodies to God as a living sacrifice (Rom 12: 1). They paid the price;
they obtained the expected reward. They were profitable for the gospel and the
advancement of Christ kingdom.  Are we
willing to pay the price? For those who are craving to be used of God, for
those who wants to experience the miracle power of God working through them,
this is the question they must ponder. 

Yet, the key to
the supernatural, the key to winning the soul of tens of thousands and millions
for Christ in this crooked and lost generation is found only in spiritual
renewal and re-awakening. We must re-order our priorities. We must re-examine
ourselves and the way we do things. We must admit to God that we have sinned
against Him. We have let Him down. False ''Prosperity Gospel'' of 'name it and
claim it Preachers' has replaced the true Gospel of our Lord Jesus Christ in
many Churches.  We have allowed ourselves
to be lured into sleep by the enemy and we must repent. God is faithful and He
will forgive us. He will remember no more our transgressions against Him if
only we will repent and turn back to Him. Look at what He says in 2 Chronicles
7:14: " *If my people that are called by my name will humble themselves and pray
and seek my face and turn from their wicked ways, will I HEAR FROM HEAVEN AND
WILL FORGIVE THEIR SINS AND HEAL THEIR LAND".* When God talks about
healing, He means total restoration. This includes physical, spiritual,
emotional, mental healing etc. Every facet of our lives will receive healing.
We need to start praying seriously, seeking his face and turn away from our
worldly cravings. The scriptures clearly warned us not to love the World, which
will eventually be destroyed by fire. If anybody professing to be a Christian
loves the World and the material things of this World, the love of God, the
father is not in him (1 John 2:15 - 17). 

The Holy Spirit
will not fill an unclean vessel. The Holy Spirit will only fill and equip with
power a ready and willing person. Whatever God is going to do in these last
days is going to start in the House of God.  So, let's get ready for the coming latter
rain. Let's get ourselves fully prepared. Remember the early Apostles and other
Christians waited on God in the upper room for the promised baptism of the Holy
Spirit (Acts 1:4-5). They were pure, holy and fully sanctified to God. That is,
they have been separated unto the Lord before the actual Holy Spirit Baptism
and power came upon them. God is the same today, yesterday and forever (Heb
13:8). So, if this is so, God does not expect anything less from us. From time
as far back as the time of Old Testament, before the people meet God or God
comes into their midst, they must be consecrated unto the Lord. Without this,
nothing will happen. So therefore, Brethren let's get rid of our cold, lukewarm
and complacent attitude. We need to re-surrender and rededicate our lives afresh
to God in obedience.   

BY: Jonathan Taiwo (GOLEM Director of
Overseas Missions).
